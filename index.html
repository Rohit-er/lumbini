Maya Devi Temple

Central Lumbini

Loading image...

The Maya Devi Temple is the most sacred site in Lumbini, marking the exact spot where Queen Mayadevi gave birth to Siddhartha Gautama in 623 BCE. The temple complex houses the Marker Stone, which pinpoints the birthplace, and the Nativity Sculpture depicting the birth scene. The temple stands near the sacred pond where Maya Devi bathed before giving birth, and the Ashoka Pillar erected by Emperor Ashoka in 249 BCE to commemorate his pilgrimage to this site. The temple's archaeological remains have been designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Ashoka Pillar

Near Maya Devi Temple

Erected by Emperor Ashoka in 249 BCE during his pilgrimage to Lumbini, this sandstone pillar is one of the earliest inscribed monuments in Nepal. The Brahmi script inscription confirms Lumbini as the birthplace of Buddha and commemorates Ashoka's visit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about visiting Lumbini

Do I need a special permit to visit Lumbini?

No special permit is required for international visitors to enter Lumbini. However, all foreign visitors need a valid Nepal visa which can be obtained on arrival at the airport or border crossings.

What is the best time to visit Lumbini?

The ideal time to visit Lumbini is between October and March when the weather is pleasant with minimal rainfall. Temperatures range from 15Â°C to 25Â°C during these months.

Are guided tours available at Lumbini?

Yes, certified guides are available at the entrance. We recommend booking in advance through our website for the best experience. Tours are available in English, Hindi, Nepali, Japanese, and several other languages.

Is photography allowed in the sacred garden?

Photography is permitted throughout Lumbini except in the inner sanctum of Maya Devi Temple where the exact birthplace marker is located. Please be respectful and avoid using flash photography inside temples.

How do I get to Lumbini from Kathmandu?

You can reach Lumbini by:

  • Flight: 45-minute flight to Bhairahawa Airport (25km from Lumbini)
  • Bus: 8-10 hour tourist bus ride from Kathmandu
  • Private vehicle: 6-7 hour drive via Prithvi Highway
